Unlocking the Conversation in Your App

Your web browser doesn't have a cute name like Alexa, Siri, or Cortana, but it can be just as talkative. Your smartphone, with a built-in speaker and microphone, is a perfect device for building a standards-based, browser-native, framework-free Conversational UI using nothing but W3C specifications like the Web Speech API and Speech Synthesis Markup Language (SSML).

In this workshop, Scott Davis (Web Architect and Developer Advocate, ThoughtWorks) helps you unlock the Conversational UI that is already in your app or website.

  • We'll discuss the semantics of HTML and the Accessibility (including text-to-speech) baked into every modern browser, desktop or mobile.
  • We'll add JavaScript-based SpeechSynthesis to your app in two lines of code.
  • For more sophisticated speech, we'll explore Speech Synthesis Markup Language (SSML) -- the markup language used by Amazon Alexa, Apple Siri, Microsoft Cortana, and others.
  • We'll leverage SpeechRecognition (speech-to-text) available to you in your browser, so that you can talk to your browser.
  • And finally, we'll add closed captions to your videos (using WebVTT) and transcripts to your podcasts to help with Search Engine Optimization and making the Accessibility of your web app more robust.
